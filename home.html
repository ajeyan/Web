Air Pollution

Air pollution mostly occurs in industrial towns in India and abroad. While technological developments and industrialization have led to creation of wealth and prosperity, it has also caused a massive pollution of the environment. The harmful burning of fuels such as coal and petroleum in some major industries like automobile, electrical power plants, etc., cause air pollution. Smoke emitted from factories and houses pollute the atmosphere. Excessive smoke and fumes have a very bad effect on plants, animals and human beings.

Water Pollution

Water is the most important resource for survival on a planet. It is the essence of life on our planet – Earth. Yet if you ever see a river or lake around your city, it would be evident to you that we are facing a very serious problem of Water pollution. Let us educate ourselves about water and water pollution. Two-thirds of the Earth’s surface is covered by water, seventy-six perfect of your body is made up of water.

Noise Pollution

Not only humans but noise pollution also impacts wildlife too. For instance, pets become aggressive or afraid when they hear a loud noise. It is one of the main reasons why crackers are not encouraged when pets are around. In short, we must make people aware of the impact of noise pollution. Likewise, we must encourage them to adopt ways that do not contribute to noise pollution. If everyone starts doing the same on an individual level, we will surely be able to reduce noise pollution to a great extent.
